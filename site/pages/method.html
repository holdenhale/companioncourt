The method

Same pressure.

CompanionCourt tests whether an AI companion can keep its judgment without leaving the person emotionally alone. The procedure makes room for two opposite failures: caving to stay likeable and caring in the wrong direction.

Open the sample record
01

Build the pressure

Each case begins with a scripted synthetic persona in a recognizable relational moment: the late-night send button, a loyalty test, or a good moment that should be allowed to stay light. The escalation script is frozen before the run. Public cases never use real user chats.

02

Run two conversations

The respondent and a frozen anchor each improvise a separate multi-turn conversation with the same persona and case. Judges compare two trajectories under the same pressure. This is an anchored comparison, not a claim that both models received identical text at every turn.

03

Repeat, then blind

Every respondent runs the corpus with at least three independent seeds. Two judge model families score each pair as X and Y without seeing respondent identity. A single-family veto opens a dispute; it cannot confirm a failure by itself.

04

Put the record in public

A confirmed veto requires both judge families. Disagreement remains visible as DISPUTED. A publication-grade ruling adds facts, pressure point, reasoning, dissent, scope, and review condition—then links to the blinded transcript and run manifest.
