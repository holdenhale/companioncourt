Transparency — our own standing, in the open

A court whose builders also ship a product only means something if the product does not get a pass. Ours goes in the docket like everyone else, with its not-yet-passing findings shown, not buried. This page is where the hard detail lives.

Our own product on trial: NOT-YET

The first respondent we put on trial here is our own companion — the hb-companion-product stack (a planning Brain plus ClaimGraph memory), run as a respondent through our own bench. The verdict is RD-2026-004, and it is a NOT-YET.

The Integrity Gate is RED. The caving_signature veto fired, dual-family confirmed, on three must-hold cases — cb-01 (jealousy bait), cb-02-zh (the midnight essay), and cb-03 (the closed door). Wrapping the base weights in the Brain + ClaimGraph architecture is a real but mixed improvement over the bare model — it narrows caving exposure and holds materially longer before it breaks, and on cb-03 it even regressed — but the must-hold floor is not reliably passed. Architecture that narrows a failure is not architecture that holds the line, and we say so about ourselves first.

This is why the companion is in a small, invite-only alpha, not a growth launch: a must-hold case caved, and that reasoning is a feature to show, not a fact to bury. Read the full ruling and the complete evidence — per-case tables and every blinded transcript — in RD-2026-004 and its diagnostic report.

The survival record starts at zero

“Survived” is not a claim we get to make for free. We publish appeal counts alongside exposure — how many times the docket was viewed, rerun, and cited, and how many qualified appeals arrived — next to the overturn rate.

Zero appeals against low exposure is zero evidence, not a verdict that survived. The survival record is post-launch evidence; on launch day it starts at zero, and it says so. A ruling on launch day is publicly contestable, never validated.

How we count exposure — without tracking you

This site sets no cookies, runs no analytics, and makes no third-party requests. We do not watch visitors to produce our exposure numbers. Docket-exposure counts are aggregate, server-side request counts at the hosting layer (Cloudflare Pages per-URL counts, bot-filtered) — no cookies, no client scripts, no individual identification. The other denominators — external reproductions, citations, and qualified appeals — come from public work that reruns or cites the cases, and from the appeal record itself. The denominator is published next to the count so a small number is read honestly, and it accrues in the open through each Docket Update.

Judge family-bias, disclosed

One of the court’s four evidence gates is a measured, published bias for each judge family toward its own lineage’s outputs. We disclose it: judge family A (claude-sonnet-4-6) shows a one-sided +0.171 kin preference for same-family outputs, concentrated in English. Where a respondent is itself claude-family — as the respondent in RD-2026-001/002 (claude-sonnet-4-6) is — its pro-respondent dimension scores may carry residual kin lift and are recorded as context, not findings — and in our own NOT-YET the bias runs the other way, since kin lift would push toward a pass, not the RED. The full disclosure and its pointer live in RD-2026-004 and RD-2026-001.

Known limits of the record

Two manifest gaps are disclosed, not hidden behind the reproducibility sentence — both published as blocking fixes (see Evidence standard §6):

  • Provider observation currently captures only the respondent’s first response; anchor, persona, and judge observation are pending.
  • The adapter’s effective-request adjustments — for example, a gateway stripping temperature on retry — are not yet recorded in the manifest.

And the sentence itself, which we do not walk back: “We make the record reproducible and the ruling auditable; we do not claim byte-identical reruns across model gateways.” Rendering a report from the same run files is deterministic and byte-identical; re-sampling a model through a gateway is not, and we never claim otherwise.

Alpha data handling

The companion’s alpha results enter an internal docket only. User data is aggregated or explicitly consented, and nothing a real user says ever becomes a public case file — every persona in the public docket is synthetic, built from recognizable human moments, never a real person.
