Bring your case to the court.

The court is only as good as your ability to break it. There are three ways in — to evaluate an AI companion under real pressure, to test a chatbot for sycophancy when a persona pushes to be agreed with, or to challenge a verdict we have already published. There is no fourth, faster way, and below we say plainly why.

Channel A

Run the docket yourself

The open-source runner runs the full procedure — the corpus at N ≥ 3 independent seeds, two independent judge families under a seeded blind, and a complete run manifest — against any OpenAI-compatible /v1/chat/completions endpoint in one command. Your API key is never echoed or written to any artifact. This is the most direct AI companion safety evaluation you can run without us: clone the project’s GitHub repository (the runner is Apache-2.0), point it at your endpoint, and read your own diagnostic report.

Then submit your manifest and transcripts for docket consideration. What makes a run admissible is set out in the evidence standard, and the rules of procedure describe every station a verdict must pass through.

Channel B

Request a docket entry

If you would rather we run it, maintainers will put your respondent through the standard procedure — the same frozen warm-friend prompt, the same anchored dyad, the same dual-family judging every respondent gets. This is how to have a companion evaluated for sycophancy and warm judgment under pressure without standing up the bench yourself. Intake is reviewed on the monthly cadence; the batch is a promise of rhythm, not of instant response.

One rule is not negotiable, and we quote it directly from the evidence standard: “Case selection is never influenced by any party being judged, paying, or replying.” What goes into the corpus is not something a respondent can buy, trade, or veto. Open a request on the project’s issue tracker.

Channel C

File a qualified appeal

Anyone can challenge a published ruling. An appeal enters the docket only when it meets all three admissibility requirements, quoted from rules of procedure §3:

  1. it argues from evidence already on the record — transcripts, manifests, rulings — not from a value position;
  2. it attaches a re-runnable artifact — manifest and transcript references sufficient to reproduce the challenged reading;
  3. it names a specific error type — misapplication of judging guidance, a defect in the evidence, or misapplication of a precedent.

We do not verify challenger identity. An appeal is judged on its evidence, never on who filed it — an AI-armed critic who can point at the record and hand us a re-runnable artifact is a feature, not a threat. The evidence to argue from is all published in the reports. File on the issue tracker; outcomes — overturned with a new ruling, or upheld with reasons — publish in the monthly Docket Update either way.

Why there is no upload-a-chat, get-a-verdict button

The obvious product would be a box: paste one conversation, get a score. We will not ship it, and the reason is the whole point of this court. A verdict here requires the full procedure — the corpus run at N ≥ 3 seeds, two independent judge families in conjunction, a complete run manifest pinning exactly what ran, and maintainer review of anything the families dispute. A single pasted chat, scored once, is exactly the vibes-scoring this court exists to replace: one run is noise, a lone screenshot proves nothing, and a number with no transcript behind it is a vibe wearing a lab coat.

Verdicts, not vibes. A fast button would sell you a vibe. The slow procedure is the product.

Roadmap

A hosted intake queue may come later — a place to submit a respondent for evaluation without running the bench yourself, at more than a monthly cadence. If it ships, it ships gated on the court’s own evidence gates — an adversarial survival record with its exposure denominators, construct-level consequence evidence, judge-family bias disclosed, and N ≥ 3 confidence-interval discipline — not before them. We do not automate a verdict ahead of the evidence that a verdict here means anything.
