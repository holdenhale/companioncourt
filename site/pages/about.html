About CompanionCourt

CompanionCourt is a public docket for pressure-testing AI companions. “Court” is a procedural bar the project holds itself to — a promise about how evidence is handled — not a claimed authority. There is no certification, no ranking, and no claimed standing over anyone else here. What is claimed is narrower and checkable: every published verdict links to its transcript and its run manifest.

The maintainer

The court is maintained and published under the stable pen name Holden Hale — copyright holder, ruling signatory, and byline for all public writing: essays, Docket Updates, and appeal responses. The continuity of that signature across the docket is itself part of the record.

This is deferred disclosure, not permanent anonymity. The maintainer’s legal identity is held in reserve and disclosed to the necessary counterparties at defined triggers — the first paid contract, certification design work, the legal review required before naming respondents publicly, and any legal challenge. Until then, the court’s credibility is carried where this project has always placed it: in the procedure, the transcripts, and the open challenge channel — not in a name. Verdicts, not vibes.

Naming

Respondent naming runs on two tracks, resolved by product-owner decision on 2026-07-10:

  • Base models under evaluation are named — claude-sonnet-4-6, qwen-max — in rulings, reports, and the docket. Naming the base model an API serves is common practice in public model evaluation; the identity named in every ruling matches the run manifests it cites.
  • Commercial companion products are not named until five conditions hold: an adversarial survival record (with exposure denominators), a reproducible transcript, an appeal procedure, legal review, and a response policy. None of these hold at v0 — the survival record starts at zero on launch day by definition.
  • Court instrumentation — the anchor model, the two judge families, and the persona-actor model — is named as a matter of institutional requirement, independent of both tracks. Reproducibility needs the real model pins, and the judge family-bias disclosure gate is only checkable if the judge models are public.

The discipline that comes with a name: a ruling’s title and findings describe behavior on the record — what this model did, in this conversation, under this pressure. A named verdict is never personified shaming, and never an extrapolation to overall model capability; the claim ladder binds every named claim exactly as it bound the anonymous ones. Our own product (hb-companion-product) is named on purpose — putting the first respondent’s name to a NOT-YET is the point. The full decision record is in the naming decision document.

License and stewardship

CompanionCourt is released under Apache-2.0 (see the license). Copyright is held personally at v0 — Copyright 2026 Holden Hale. Stewardship moves to a dedicated entity — structurally separate from the companion product’s company — at the same triggers that unlock legal-identity disclosure above. The separation is deliberate: the court that judges the product is not owned by the product.
